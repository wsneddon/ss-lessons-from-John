1. Biblical Chronology and Genealogies

Literal reading of Genesis 1–11 (creation in six 24-hour days, global flood ~4,300–4,500 years ago).
Genealogies in Genesis 5 and 11 provide a continuous timeline from Adam to Abraham, which, when added to known historical dates, places creation around 4000 BC.
Jesus and New Testament writers referring to Genesis as literal history.

2. Rapid Geological Formations

Polystrate fossils: Trees and animal remains found vertically spanning multiple sedimentary layers supposedly deposited over millions of years.
Fossil graveyards: Massive concentrations of rapidly buried creatures (e.g., dinosaur bone beds, fish shoals).
Soft tissue in dinosaur fossils: Original proteins, blood vessels, and cells in bones dated to 65+ million years old (e.g., Mary Schweitzer’s T. rex finds).
Rapid canyon formation: Observations of canyons (e.g., Mount St. Helens 1980 eruption) forming in days/weeks, not millions of years.
Flat gaps / paraconformities: Missing supposed millions of years with little erosion between layers.

3. Radiometric Dating Issues

Inconsistent dates: Different radiometric methods on the same rock often give wildly different ages.
Excess helium in zircons: Zircon crystals contain high levels of helium from uranium decay—helium diffuses out quickly, suggesting only thousands of years of decay, not billions (RATE project findings).
Carbon-14 in “ancient” materials: Detectable C-14 in diamonds, coal, and fossils claimed to be millions of years old (C-14 half-life ~5,730 years limits it to <50,000–100,000 years max).
Accelerated decay evidence: Proposals of past periods of rapid nuclear decay during creation week or the Flood.

4. Biological and Genetic Evidence

Human population growth: Current population growth rates, if extrapolated backward, fit a starting population of ~8 people (Noah’s family) ~4,500 years ago better than millions of years.
Genetic entropy / mutation load: Accumulation of harmful mutations suggests genomes are degrading rapidly, inconsistent with deep time (arguments by John Sanford).
Living fossils: Many species (coelacanth, horseshoe crab, etc.) appear unchanged from fossils claimed to be hundreds of millions of years old.
Lack of transitional forms: Perceived scarcity or controversy over clear evolutionary intermediates in the fossil record.

5. Astronomical and Cosmological Arguments

Comet lifetimes: Short-period comets lose material quickly and should not exist after millions of years without a constant source (Oort cloud hypothesized but unobserved).
Magnetic field decay: Earth’s magnetic field is decaying at a measurable rate; extrapolating backward, it would have been impossibly strong only tens of thousands of years ago.
Spiral galaxy arms: Galaxies should wind up tightly after a few hundred million years, yet many retain clear spiral structure.
Moon recession: The Moon is moving away from Earth; backward extrapolation suggests it would have been catastrophically close to Earth within ~1–2 billion years (not 4.5 billion).

6. Salt in the Ocean

Sodium input into oceans from rivers exceeds removal rates; at current rates, oceans should be much saltier if Earth were billions of years old.

7. Sediment and Erosion Rates

Continents would erode to sea level in ~10–20 million years at current rates.
Ocean sediment thickness is far less than expected for hundreds of millions of years of accumulation.

8. Historical and Cultural Evidence

Many ancient cultures have flood legends remarkably similar to Noah’s Flood.
Early historical records and civilizations appear suddenly around 3000–4000 BC with advanced knowledge, no clear evidence of millions of years of primitive development.

These points are frequently discussed in young-Earth creationist literature (e.g., Institute for Creation Research, Answers in Genesis, Creation Ministries International). Mainstream science generally interprets the same data through an old-Earth framework and offers counter-explanations (e.g., contamination for C-14, different decay rates in the past not occurring, etc.).
Proponents argue the evidence is better explained by a recent creation and global catastrophic flood rather than uniformitarian deep time.
