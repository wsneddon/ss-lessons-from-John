Opening Song
Introduction — Why I Think This Matters

The lessons up to this point have focused on the core, non-negotiable doctrines of the Christian faith — beliefs like the deity of Christ, where we can say plainly: if you reject this, you are rejecting Christianity itself.

Today's lesson is different. Some of the Scriptures we'll look at are interpreted differently by sincere, Bible-believing Christians, and I want to be upfront about that. I have come to believe in a young earth and a literal six-day creation. I'm also convinced that God did not use billions of years of death and suffering to create a world He called "very good." I hold these views with real conviction — but as my mother used to remind me, "Bill, just remember you don't know everything."

My goal today is not to win an argument. If you leave unconvinced of a young earth, I'd simply hope you could say: "I can see how an intelligent, Bible-believing Christian might come to that conclusion." Young earth believers are too often dismissed — even by fellow Christians — as naive or anti-intellectual. I hope this lesson pushes back on that a little, and that we can discuss it the way brothers and sisters in Christ should.

E. Physical Evidence Consistent with a Young Earth
1. Evidence for Noah's Flood and Rapid Geological Formations
a. Ancient flood legends: Many cultures worldwide have flood legends remarkably similar to the account of Noah's Flood.
b. Early civilizations: Historical records and civilizations appear suddenly around 3000–4000 BC with advanced knowledge, with no evidence of millions of years of gradual primitive development.
c. Polystrate fossils: Trees and animal remains found vertically spanning multiple sedimentary layers supposedly deposited over millions of years.
i. Example image
d. Fossil graveyards: Massive concentrations of rapidly buried creatures (e.g., dinosaur bone beds, fish shoals).
e. Soft tissue in dinosaur fossils: Original proteins, blood vessels, and cells in bones dated to 65+ million years old (e.g., Mary Schweitzer's T. rex finds).
f. Rapid canyon formation: Canyons (e.g., Mount St. Helens 1980 eruption) forming in days or weeks, not millions of years.
g. Flat gaps / paraconformities: Missing supposed millions of years with little to no erosion between sedimentary layers. Video
h. Great Missoula Flood: Long video | Lake Missoula flood — Creationist interpretation
i. Bent rock layers without fracturing: Sedimentary strata tightly folded without the heat/pressure needed to deform hardened rock — suggesting layers were still soft when folded. Example image
2. Radiometric Dating Issues
a. Inconsistent dates: Different radiometric methods applied to the same rock often yield wildly different ages.
b. Excess helium in zircons: High helium levels from uranium decay suggest only thousands of years of decay rather than billions (RATE project).
c. Carbon-14 in "ancient" materials: Detectable C-14 in diamonds, coal, and fossils claimed to be millions of years old (C-14 half-life ~5,730 years limits detection to <100,000 years).
d. Accelerated decay evidence: Proposals of rapid nuclear decay during the creation week or the Flood.
3. Biological and Genetic Evidence
a. Human population growth: Current growth rates extrapolated backward fit a starting population of ~8 people (Noah's family) ~4,500 years ago.
b. Genetic entropy / mutation load: Accumulation of harmful mutations suggests genomes are degrading rapidly, inconsistent with deep time (John Sanford).
c. Living fossils: Species (e.g., coelacanth, horseshoe crab) essentially unchanged from fossils claimed to be hundreds of millions of years old.
d. Lack of transitional forms: Ongoing controversy over clear evolutionary intermediates in the fossil record.
4. Astronomical and Cosmological Arguments
a. Comet lifetimes: Short-period comets lose material quickly and should not exist after millions of years without a constant source (Oort cloud hypothesized but unobserved).
b. Magnetic field decay: Earth's magnetic field is decaying at a measurable rate; extrapolating backward, it would have been impossibly strong only tens of thousands of years ago.
c. Spiral galaxy arms: Galaxies should wind up tightly after a few hundred million years, yet many retain clear spiral structure.
d. Moon recession: The Moon is moving away from Earth; backward extrapolation suggests catastrophically close proximity within ~1–2 billion years (not 4.5 billion).
5. Salt in the Ocean
a. Sodium input into the oceans from rivers exceeds removal rates. At current rates, the oceans should be much saltier if Earth were billions of years old.
6. Sediment and Erosion Rates
a. Continents would erode down to sea level in roughly 10–20 million years at current erosion rates.
b. Ocean sediment thickness is far less than expected for hundreds of millions of years of accumulation.
