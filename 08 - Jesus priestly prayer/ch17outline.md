**John 17** is known as Jesus' **High Priestly Prayer**. It is the longest recorded prayer of Jesus in the Gospels and occurs just before His arrest, as He prepares to face the cross. In it, Jesus prays first for Himself, then for His immediate disciples, and finally for all future believers.

Here is a clear breakdown of the **major themes**, drawn directly from the chapter (using NIV/ESV wording for reference):

### 1. **Glorification of the Father and the Son** (vv. 1-5)
- Jesus prays, “Father, the hour has come. Glorify your Son, that your Son may glorify you.”
- He has completed the work the Father gave Him and now asks to be restored to the pre-existent glory He shared with the Father “before the world began.”
- **Eternal life** is defined here as knowing “the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom you have sent.”
- **Key idea**: The cross is not defeat but the means by which both Father and Son are glorified through obedience, revelation, and redemption.

### 2. **Revelation and Transmission of God’s Word/Truth** (vv. 6-8, 14, 17)
- Jesus has manifested the Father’s name and given the disciples the words the Father gave Him.
- They have received, believed, and known that Jesus came from the Father.
- **Sanctification** comes “by the truth; your word is truth.”
- **Key idea**: Discipleship is rooted in receiving and abiding in God’s revealed truth.

### 3. **Protection and Preservation of Believers** (vv. 9-15)
- Jesus prays specifically for those the Father has given Him (not for the world).
- He asks the Father to “keep them in your name” / “protect them by the power of your name” so they may be one.
- “I do not ask that you take them out of the world, but that you keep them from the evil one.”
- **Key idea**: Believers are kept in the world for a purpose but need divine protection from evil and division.

### 4. **Unity of Believers** (vv. 11, 20-23)
- “That they may be one as we are one” — modeled on the perfect unity of the Father and Son.
- This unity extends to all future believers who will believe through the disciples’ message.
- The goal: “so that the world may believe that you have sent me” and “know that you have loved them even as you have loved me.”
- **Key idea**: Visible, relational unity among Christians (rooted in truth, love, and shared life in God) serves as powerful witness to the world.

### 5. **Love, Joy, and Shared Glory** (vv. 13, 22-26)
- Jesus wants His joy to be fulfilled in His followers.
- He has given them the glory the Father gave Him.
- He desires that believers be with Him to see His glory and that the Father’s love for the Son would be in them, with Jesus Himself in them.
- **Key idea**: The Christian life participates in the love, joy, and glory of the Triune God.

### Overall Structure and Purpose
The prayer moves outward in concentric circles:
- **Jesus Himself** (glorification through the cross).
- **The immediate disciples** (protection, sanctification, joy).
- **All future believers** (unity and mission to the world).

**John 17** reveals the heart of Jesus as our great High Priest: His concern for God’s glory, the security of His people, their holiness, their loving unity, and their effectiveness in mission. It is both deeply intimate and profoundly missional. Many Christians return to this chapter repeatedly for encouragement in prayer, unity, and perseverance.
