# Light and Life in Scripture

**John 1:4-5**  
“In him was life, and the life was the light of men. The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it.”

## Key Greek and Hebrew Words

### New Testament (Greek)
- **Light** — φῶς (*phōs*): Spiritual truth, revelation, purity, and divine presence. Contrasts with σκότος/σκοτία (*skotos/skotia*) = darkness (ignorance, sin, evil).
- **Life** — ζωή (*zōē*): Divine, eternal, abundant life — the very life of God Himself (not merely biological existence).

### Old Testament (Hebrew)
- **Light** — אוֹר (*ʾôr*): Illumination, guidance, joy, salvation, and God’s presence.
- **Life** — חַיִּים (*chayyim*): Vitality, full and abundant life (often plural).

## Scriptures: God / Jesus / Spirit / Word as **Light**

- **God the Father**  
  - 1 John 1:5 — “God is light, and in him is no darkness at all.”  
  - Psalm 27:1 — “The Lord is my light and my salvation.”  
  - Psalm 36:9 — “In your light do we see light.”

- **Jesus (the Word)**  
  - John 8:12 — “I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life.”  
  - John 1:9 — “The true light, which gives light to everyone, was coming into the world.”  
  - John 9:5; 12:46; Revelation 21:23

- **The Holy Spirit**  
  The Spirit illuminates truth (John 14:26; 1 Corinthians 2:10-12). Believers are called to “walk as children of light” (Ephesians 5:8).

- **The Word of God**  
  - Psalm 119:105 — “Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path.”  
  - Psalm 119:130 — “The unfolding of your words gives light.”

**Other verses**: Isaiah 60:19-20; Matthew 5:14-16 (believers as light).

## Scriptures: God / Jesus / Spirit / Word as **Life**

- **God the Father**  
  - Psalm 36:9 — “With you is the fountain of life.”  
  - Acts 17:25 — God gives to all mankind life and breath.

- **Jesus**  
  - John 1:4 — “In him was life.”  
  - John 14:6 — “I am the way, the truth, and the life.”  
  - John 5:26; 6:35, 48 (“bread of life”); 10:10; 11:25 (“resurrection and the life”).

- **The Holy Spirit**  
  - John 6:63 — “It is the Spirit who gives life.”  
  - 2 Corinthians 3:6 — “The Spirit gives life.”

- **The Word of God**  
  - John 6:63 — “The words that I have spoken to you are spirit and life.”  
  - Deuteronomy 8:3 / Matthew 4:4 — “Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word from the mouth of God.”

## The Relationship Between Light and Life

In Scripture, **light and life** are deeply connected:

1. **Life produces Light**  
   The divine *zōē* in the Word **is** the light of humanity (John 1:4). Jesus’ eternal life is the source of all spiritual illumination.

2. **Light leads to and sustains Life**  
   Following the light brings the “light of life” (John 8:12). Light reveals truth, exposes sin, and guides to salvation.

3. **Key Theological Themes**
   - Creation parallel: God speaks light into darkness, bringing order and life (Genesis 1 → John 1).
   - Victory over darkness: The light shines and is not overcome (John 1:5).
   - Believers’ calling: We become “children of light” (Ephesians 5:8) and reflect Jesus’ light (Matthew 5:14-16).
   - Eternal hope: In the new creation, “the Lord God will be their light, and the Lamb is its lamp” (Revelation 21:23; 22:5).

**Christian Thought Summary**:  
Light and life are two sides of the same divine reality in Christ. Light dispels ignorance and moral darkness; life imparts the power to live rightly and eternally. Early church fathers and theologians (Augustine, Calvin, etc.) saw Jesus as both the source of existence (*life*) and revelation (*light*).
