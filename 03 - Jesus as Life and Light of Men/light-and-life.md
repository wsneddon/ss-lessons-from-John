# Light and Life in Scripture

**John 1:4–5**
> "In him was life, and the life was the light of men. The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it."

This theme runs from Genesis to Revelation and is foundational to biblical theology.

---

## Key Greek and Hebrew Words

### Old Testament (Hebrew)

- **Light — אוֹר (*ʾôr*)**: Illumination, guidance, joy, salvation, and God's presence.
- **Life — חַיִּים (*ḥayyîm*)**: Vitality, full and abundant life; often covenantal blessing (from root *ḥayah* — "to live").
- **Breath of Life — נְשָׁמָה (*neshamah*)**: Life-giving breath from God (Genesis 2:7 — God breathes life into Adam).

### New Testament (Greek)

- **Light — φῶς (*phōs*)**: Spiritual truth, revelation, purity, divine presence. Contrasts with σκότος (*skotos*) = darkness (ignorance, sin, evil).
- **Life — ζωή (*zōē*)**: Divine, eternal, abundant life — the very life of God (distinct from βίος/*bios* = biological life).
- **Word — λόγος (*logos*)**: Word, reason, divine expression — key in John 1.
- **Spirit — πνεῦμα (*pneuma*)**: Spirit, breath, wind — associated with giving life.

## The Relationship Between Light and Life

Light and life are not separate themes — they are deeply unified in Scripture.

**Psalm 36:9** is the foundational linking passage:
> "With you is the fountain of life; in your light do we see light."

### John's Theology (John 1:4)
- **Life (zōē)** = Divine essence — the source
- **Light (phōs)** = Divine revelation — the expression
- Life produces light; light reveals life.

### A Repeating Biblical Pattern
1. God gives **life**
2. Life produces **light**
3. Light defeats **darkness**
4. Darkness represents **death**

Seen in: Genesis 1 → Psalm 36 → John 1 → Revelation 21–22

### Creation Parallel
- Genesis 1: Light created first; life follows.
- John 1: Word → Life → Light.
- Both show God bringing order and life out of darkness and void.

### Eternal Fulfillment
- Revelation 21:23 — "The glory of God gives it light, and its lamp is the Lamb."
- Revelation 22:1–2 — River of life, tree of life, eternal light — life and light completely united.
- Isaiah 60:19–20 anticipates this: God as everlasting light.

### Believers' Calling
- Matthew 5:14–16 — "You are the light of the world."
- Ephesians 5:8 — "Walk as children of light."

## Christian Thought

**Athanasius** (*On the Incarnation*, 4th c.) — Christ restores life by bringing light; darkness represents corruption and death; light restores creation.

**Augustine** (4th–5th c.) — Light = truth and knowledge of God; ignorance = darkness; knowledge of God = light; union with God = life.

**John Calvin** — Light → revelation; Life → regeneration. The Spirit provides both illumination and new life.
