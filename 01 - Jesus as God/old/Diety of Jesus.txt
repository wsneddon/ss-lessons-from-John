A. Seed of the Woman (Genesis 3:15 and Fulfillments)
  Old Testament Prophecy:

  Genesis 3:15 (ESV):
    “I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and her offspring; he shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise his heel.”
    (The singular "he/his" points to one ultimate offspring who defeats evil, unlike the plural "your offspring/her offspring" for general enmity.)
  
  New Testament Fulfillments / Allusions:
  
    Galatians 4:4 —
    “But when the fullness of time had come, God sent forth his Son, born of woman, born under the law…”
    (Direct echo: Jesus is the one "born of woman" to redeem humanity, fulfilling the seed promise.)
    Romans 16:20 —
    “The God of peace will soon crush Satan under your feet.”
    (Alludes to Genesis 3:15's head-crushing; applied to believers through Christ, who achieved the victory.)
    Hebrews 2:14 —
    “…that through death he might destroy the one who has the power of death, that is, the devil…”
    (Jesus' death/resurrection crushes the serpent's power.)
    Revelation 12:9, 17 —
    “…that ancient serpent, who is called the devil and Satan…” (v.9); “…the dragon became furious… and went off to make war on… the rest of her offspring…” (v.17).
    (Ties back to Genesis 3:15's enmity, with Jesus as the primary seed and believers as part of her offspring.)
  
    This emphasizes Jesus' human birth (via virgin Mary, no earthly father) to conquer sin/Satan.


B. Seed of Abraham
   Old Testament Prophecy
   
     Genesis 22:18 (and parallels like Genesis 12:3; 17:19):
     “…and in your offspring shall all the nations of the earth be blessed…”
     (Promises blessings to all peoples through Abraham's seed.)
   
   New Testament Fulfillments
   
     Galatians 3:16 —
     “Now the promises were made to Abraham and to his offspring. It does not say, ‘And to offsprings,’ referring to many, but referring to one, ‘And to your   offspring,’    who is Christ.”
     (Paul explicitly identifies Jesus as the singular seed through whom the promises come.)
     Galatians 3:29 —
     “And if you are Christ’s, then you are Abraham’s offspring, heirs according to promise.”
     (Believers share in the promise through union with Christ, the true seed.)
     Matthew 1:1 —
     “The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham.”
     (Genealogy traces Jesus' legal descent from Abraham, fulfilling the lineage promise.)
     Luke 3:34 (in Jesus' genealogy) —
     “…the son of Abraham…”
     (Physical/human descent affirmed

D. Verses About Melchizedek (Old Testament Type of Christ’s Eternal Priesthood & Deity)
  
  Melchizedek is a mysterious king-priest who suddenly appears with no genealogy, receives tithes, and blesses Abraham. Psalm 110 and Hebrews use him as a picture of  Jesus’ eternal, non-Levitical priesthood—something only possible for God Himself.
  
  Old Testament
  
      Genesis 14:18–20
      “And Melchizedek king of Salem brought out bread and wine. (He was priest of God Most High.) And he blessed him… And Abram gave him a tenth of everything.”
      Psalm 110:4
      “The Lord has sworn and will not change his mind: ‘You are a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek.’”
  
  New Testament Application (Hebrews)
  
      Hebrews 7:1–3
      “For this Melchizedek… having neither beginning of days nor end of life, but resembling the Son of God he continues a priest forever.”
      Hebrews 7:15–17
      “…he arises in the likeness of Melchizedek… ‘You are a priest forever, after the order of Melchizedek.’”
      Hebrews 7:24–25
      “But he holds his priesthood permanently, because he continues forever… he is able to save to the uttermost…”

E. Verses About the “Son of Man” (Old Testament Prophecy of Divine Ruler)

Old Testament
 
     Daniel 7:13–14
     “I saw in the night visions, and behold, with the clouds of heaven there came one like a son of man, and he came to the Ancient of Days… And to him was given dominion and glory and a kingdom, that all peoples, nations, and languages should serve him; his dominion is an everlasting dominion…”
 
 New Testament (Jesus Claims This Divine Title)
 
     Matthew 26:64 (Jesus on trial)
     “Jesus said… ‘from now on you will see the Son of Man seated at the right hand of Power and coming on the clouds of heaven.’”
     (The high priest tore his robes and cried “blasphemy!” because this claim combines Daniel 7’s divine figure with God’s own cloud-riding imagery.)

G. Gospel Accounts of People Declaring Jesus' Deity or Worshiping Him
(Direct verbal declarations of divine titles like "Lord and my God," "Son of God," or acts of worship [proskuneō in Greek, implying divine reverence]. Jesus accepts these without rebuke.)

    John 20:28 (Thomas' direct declaration of deity)
    “Thomas answered him, ‘My Lord and my God!’”
    (Clearest verbal affirmation of Jesus as God; Jesus blesses belief in v. 29.)
    Matthew 16:16 (Peter's confession at Caesarea Philippi)
    “Simon Peter replied, ‘You are the Christ, the Son of the living God.’”
    (Jesus affirms this as revelation from the Father [v. 17]; parallels in Mark 8:29 and Luke 9:20 below.)
    Matthew 14:33 (Disciples in the boat after Jesus walks on water)
    “And those in the boat worshiped him, saying, ‘Truly you are the Son of God.’”
    (Group worship + divine sonship confession; follows Peter's water-walking attempt.)
    John 1:41 (Andrew's early declaration)
    “He first found his own brother Simon and said to him, ‘We have found the Messiah’ (which means Christ).”
    (Andrew proclaims Jesus the Messiah after John the Baptist's testimony.)
    Matthew 2:11 (The Magi)
    “…they fell down and worshiped him…” (infant Jesus as king, with divine homage gifts.)
    Matthew 28:9 (Women at the resurrection)
    “…they came up and took hold of his feet and worshiped him.”
    Matthew 28:17 (The eleven disciples at the Great Commission)
    “…they worshiped him, but some doubted.”
    John 9:38 (Man born blind after healing)
    “He said, ‘Lord, I believe.’ And he worshiped him.”

H. Gospel Passages Where People Declare Jesus as the Christ / Messiah
(Direct verbal declarations calling Jesus "the Christ" [Messiah/Anointed One], often paired with "Son of God" or similar divine titles. Expanded with more from Mark and parallels.)

   John 1:41 (Andrew to Simon Peter)
   “He first found his own brother Simon and said to him, ‘We have found the Messiah’ (which means Christ).”
   (Earliest Gospel recognition; Andrew brings Peter to Jesus.)
   Mark 8:29 (Peter's confession at Caesarea Philippi)
   “And he asked them, ‘But who do you say that I am?’ Peter answered him, ‘You are the Christ.’”
   (Mark's version is concise; Jesus charges secrecy [v. 30]. Parallel to Matthew 16:16 above.)
   Matthew 16:16 (Peter, fuller version)
   “Simon Peter replied, ‘You are the Christ, the Son of the living God.’”
   (Jesus calls this divine revelation; builds church on this truth.)
   Luke 9:20 (Peter in Luke's parallel account)
   “Then he said to them, ‘But who do you say that I am?’ And Peter answered, ‘The Christ of God.’”
   (Slight variation emphasizing God's Christ.)
   John 11:27 (Martha before raising Lazarus)
   “She said to him, ‘Yes, Lord; I believe that you are the Christ, the Son of God, who is coming into the world.’”
   (Faith confession amid hope for resurrection.)
   John 4:29, 42 (Samaritan woman and villagers)
   The woman: “Come, see a man who told me all that I ever did. Can this be the Christ?” … Villagers: “…we know that this is indeed the Savior of the world.”
   (After Jesus explicitly says “I who speak to you am he” [v. 26]; spreads belief.)
   Matthew 14:33 (Disciples after storm calms)
   “…worshiped him, saying, ‘Truly you are the Son of God.’”
   (Ties worship to messianic/divine sonship confession.)
   Mark 15:39 (Centurion at the cross)
   “And when the centurion, who stood facing him, saw that in this way he breathed his last, he said, ‘Truly this man was the Son of God!’”
   (Roman outsider's declaration at crucifixion; climactic recognition in Mark.)


I. Jesus pre-existance claims: (seem to be all in John)
    John 8:58
    “Jesus said to them, ‘Truly, truly, I say to you, before Abraham was, I am.’”
   John 17:5 (High Priestly Prayer, the night before His crucifixion)
   “And now, Father, glorify me in your own presence with the glory that I had with you before the world existed.”
   John 17:24 (same prayer, continuing)
   “Father, I desire that they also, whom you have given me, may be with me where I am, to see my glory that you have given me because you loved me before the foundation of the world.”
   → Jesus speaks of the Father's love for Him before the foundation of the world, implying eternal relational existence and shared glory predating creation.
   John 6:38
   “For I have come down from heaven, not to do my own will but the will of him who sent me.”
   → Jesus describes His origin as "from heaven," sent by the Father.
   John 6:62 (after the bread of life discourse)
   “Then what if you were to see the Son of Man ascending to where he was before?”
   → Jesus refers to returning to His prior heavenly state/existence.
   John 16:28 (Farewell Discourse)
   “I came from the Father and have come into the world, and now I am leaving the world and going to the Father.”
   → A clear cycle: from the Father → into the world → back to the Father, implying pre-incarnate presence with God.
   John 8:23 (in the same chapter as John 8:58)
   “He said to them, ‘You are from below; I am from above. You are of this world; I am not of this world.’”
   → Jesus contrasts His origin "from above" (heaven/God) with humanity's earthly origin.
   John 3:13 (to Nicodemus)
   “No one has ascended into heaven except he who descended from heaven, the Son of Man.”
   → Jesus claims unique descent from heaven, presupposing prior existence there.
