Key Parallel Episodes/Events

Testimony of John the Baptist (John 1:19–34)
	Parallels: Matthew 3:1–12; Mark 1:2–8; Luke 3:1–18 (John's preaching and testimony about Jesus as the coming one, though John omits Jesus' baptism itself).
Calling of the first disciples (John 1:35–51)
	Loose parallels: Matthew 4:18–22; Mark 1:16–20; Luke 5:1–11 (Andrew, Peter, etc., though John's version has a different sequence and includes Philip and Nathanael).
Feeding of the 5,000 (John 6:1–15)
	Parallels: Matthew 14:13–21; Mark 6:30–44; Luke 9:10–17 (the only miracle besides the resurrection that appears in all four Gospels).
Walking on the water (John 6:16–21)
	Parallels: Matthew 14:22–33; Mark 6:45–52 (Luke omits this).
Anointing at Bethany (John 12:1–8)
	Parallels: Matthew 26:6–13; Mark 14:3–9 (very close in detail; Luke 7:36–50 has a similar but likely distinct anointing by a sinful woman earlier in Jesus' ministry).
Triumphal entry into Jerusalem (John 12:12–19)
	Parallels: Matthew 21:1–11; Mark 11:1–11; Luke 19:28–44.
Cleansing of the Temple (John 2:13–22)
	Parallels: Matthew 21:12–13; Mark 11:15–17; Luke 19:45–46 (John places it early in Jesus' ministry, while the Synoptics place it late, near the Passion).
Last Supper / Prediction of betrayal (John 13:21–30)
	Parallels: Matthew 26:20–25; Mark 14:17–21; Luke 22:21–23 (John omits the institution of the Eucharist but shares the betrayal prediction).
Prediction of Peter's denial (John 13:36–38)
	Parallels: Matthew 26:31–35; Mark 14:27–31; Luke 22:31–34.
Arrest in the garden (John 18:1–11)
	Parallels: Matthew 26:47–56; Mark 14:43–52; Luke 22:47–53 (includes Malchus' ear, unique to John and Luke in different forms).
Trial before Pilate (John 18:28–19:16)
	Parallels: Matthew 27:11–26; Mark 15:1–15; Luke 23:1–25 (extended dialogue in John).
Crucifixion (John 19:16–37)
	Parallels: Matthew 27:32–56; Mark 15:21–41; Luke 23:26–49 (shared details like casting lots for clothes, though John has unique elements like "It is finished").
Burial (John 19:38–42)
	Parallels: Matthew 27:57–61; Mark 15:42–47; Luke 23:50–56.
Empty tomb discovery (John 20:1–10)
	Parallels: Matthew 28:1–10; Mark 16:1–8; Luke 24:1–12 (Mary Magdalene prominent; Peter and the other disciple running to the tomb unique to John).
Resurrection appearances (John 20:19–29; 21:1–23)
	Parallels: Luke 24:36–49 (appearance to disciples, showing wounds, peace greeting); Matthew 28:16–20 (commissioning); also elements in Mark 16:14–20 (longer ending).

Notable Shared Sayings (with conceptual or verbal parallels)

"A prophet has no honor in his own country" (John 4:44) → Mark 6:4 (and parallels in Matthew 13:57; Luke 4:24).
"Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up" (John 2:19) → Mark 14:58 (and parallels in Matthew 26:61; used at Jesus' trial).
"Whoever receives whomever I send receives me" (John 13:20) → Matthew 10:40 (and Luke 10:16).
Various sayings about belief, eternal life, or the Father-Son relationship have looser thematic echoes in Synoptic sayings.
