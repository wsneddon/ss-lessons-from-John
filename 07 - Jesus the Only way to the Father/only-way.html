VIII. Jesus: The Only Way to the Father

Opening Song
Overview Video
Opening Passage — John 14:6
John 14:6 — "I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me."
A. The Problem: Humanity's Fall into Sin
The Fall
a. Genesis 3:1 — The serpent's deception; Adam and Eve disobey God.
b. Genesis 3:8 — They hid from God.
c. Genesis 3:16–19 — Sin brought separation and curse.
d. Genesis 2:17; Romans 5:12 — Spiritual and physical death entered humanity.
Sin separates us from God
a. Isaiah 59:2 — "Your iniquities have made a separation between you and your God, and your sins have hidden his face from you."
b. Romans 3:23 — "All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."
B. God's Immediate Promise of Restoration
The Protoevangelium — First Gospel (Genesis 3:15)
a. Genesis 3:15 — "He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise his heel." — The first promise of a coming Savior who would defeat Satan at great cost to Himself.
C. The Plan Foretold in Prophecy
The suffering servant
a. Isaiah 53:4–6 — "He was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities… the Lord has laid on him the iniquity of us all."
b. Isaiah 53:10–11 — "When his soul makes an offering for guilt… Out of the anguish of his soul he shall see and be satisfied."
c. Psalm 22:1, 16–18 — "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?" — quoted by Jesus on the cross (Matthew 27:46).
d. Zechariah 12:10 — "They shall look on me, on him whom they have pierced."
D. Why the Law Could Not Save Us
The Law reveals sin but cannot justify
a. Romans 3:20 — "By works of the law no human being will be justified… through the law comes knowledge of sin."
b. Galatians 3:11 — "No one is justified before God by the law."
c. Galatians 2:21 — "If righteousness were through the law, then Christ died for no purpose."
d. James 2:10 — Breaking even one command makes us guilty of all.
E. Only Jesus Satisfies God's Justice
Propitiation — Jesus bore our punishment
a. Romans 3:23–25 — "Justified by his grace as a gift, through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus, whom God put forward as a propitiation by his blood, to be received by faith."
b. 1 Peter 3:18 — "Christ also suffered once for sins, the righteous for the unrighteous, that he might bring us to God."
c. Romans 6:23 — "The wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."
F. Jesus' Exclusive Claim — Confirmed by the Apostles
Jesus' own words
a. John 10:9 — "I am the door. If anyone enters by me, he will be saved."
b. John 3:36 — "Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life; whoever does not obey the Son shall not see life."
The apostles' testimony
a. Acts 4:12 — "There is salvation in no one else, for there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved."
G. How We Come to the Father
The way of salvation
a. Acts 20:21 — Repentance toward God and faith in Jesus.
b. John 1:12 — Receiving Him as Lord and Savior.
c. John 3:3–7 — Being born again by the Holy Spirit.
d. Ephesians 2:8–9 — "By grace you have been saved through faith… not a result of works, so that no one may boast."
H. Discussion Questions
1. Why is it significant that God promised a Savior immediately after the Fall in Genesis 3?
2. How does understanding the Law's purpose change how you see your need for Jesus?
3. What does "propitiation" mean, and why does it matter that God's justice was satisfied?
4. How do you respond to the claim that Jesus is the only way to God?
