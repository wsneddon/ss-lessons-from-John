**Jesus: The Only Way to the Father**

### Key Verse
> “Jesus said to him, ‘I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me.’”  
> — **John 14:6**

This bold statement by Jesus is one of the most important declarations in the entire Bible. It establishes that salvation and restored relationship with God are found exclusively in Him. This lesson explores why Jesus is the only way, beginning with humanity’s problem in the Garden and God’s eternal plan.

---

### 1. The Problem: Humanity’s Fall into Sin
God created humanity for perfect fellowship with Him. That relationship was shattered by sin.

> “Now the serpent was more crafty than any other beast of the field that the Lord God had made. He said to the woman, ‘Did God actually say…?’”  
> — **Genesis 3:1**

Adam and Eve disobeyed God, bringing sin and death into the world (Genesis 3:6-7). As a result:
- They hid from God (**Genesis 3:8**)
- Sin brought separation and curse (**Genesis 3:16-19**)
- Spiritual and physical death entered humanity (**Genesis 2:17**; **Romans 5:12**)

Sin creates a barrier between people and God:

> “But your iniquities have made a separation between you and your God, and your sins have hidden his face from you…”  
> — **Isaiah 59:2**

Because “all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God” (**Romans 3:23**), no one can reach the Father on their own.

---

### 2. God’s Immediate Promise of Restoration
Even in the moment of judgment, God gave the first promise of redemption — the **protoevangelium** (first gospel):

> “I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and her offspring; he shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise his heel.”  
> — **Genesis 3:15**

This points to a coming Savior (the “offspring” of the woman) who would defeat Satan, though at great cost to Himself. From this point forward, God began unfolding His plan to restore what was broken.

---

### 3. The Plan Foretold in Prophecy
God repeatedly announced through the prophets that He would send a Messiah who would suffer for sins:

- **Isaiah 53:4-6** — “Surely he has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows… But he was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities… and the Lord has laid on him the iniquity of us all.”
- **Isaiah 53:10-11** — “Yet it was the will of the Lord to crush him; he has put him to grief; when his soul makes an offering for guilt… Out of the anguish of his soul he shall see and be satisfied.”
- **Psalm 22:1, 16-18** — “My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?” (quoted by Jesus on the cross — **Matthew 27:46**)
- **Zechariah 12:10** — “They shall look on me, on him whom they have pierced…”

These prophecies show the Messiah would be a suffering servant who dies in our place — centuries before Jesus was born.

---

### 4. Why the Law Could Not Save Us
Many assume that if we just keep God’s commandments we can earn our way back to the Father. Scripture clearly says this is impossible.

> “For by works of the law no human being will be justified in his sight, since through the law comes knowledge of sin.”  
> — **Romans 3:20**

> “Now it is evident that no one is justified before God by the law…”  
> — **Galatians 3:11**

The Law was never designed to save — it was given to:
1. Reveal God’s holiness
2. Expose our sin
3. Show our need for a Savior

If the Law could have saved us, Jesus would not have needed to die:

> “For if righteousness were through the law, then Christ died for no purpose.”  
> — **Galatians 2:21**

The Law demands perfect obedience — something no sinner can deliver. Breaking even one command makes us guilty of all (**James 2:10**).

---

### 5. Only Jesus Satisfies God’s Justice
God is perfectly loving **and** perfectly just. He cannot simply overlook sin. Justice demands punishment for wrongdoing. Yet God also desires to forgive.

The solution is found at the cross. Jesus, fully God and fully man, lived a sinless life and then took our punishment:

> “For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, and are justified by his grace as a gift, through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus, whom God put forward as a propitiation by his blood, to be received by faith.”  
> — **Romans 3:23-25**

“Propitiation” means Jesus fully satisfied God’s holy wrath against sin. On the cross, Jesus took the judgment we deserved so we could be forgiven.

> “For Christ also suffered once for sins, the righteous for the unrighteous, that he might bring us to God…”  
> — **1 Peter 3:18**

> “For the wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord.”  
> — **Romans 6:23**

Only Jesus could do this. No animal sacrifice, no amount of good works, and no religious effort could satisfy divine justice. Only the sinless Son of God could.

---

### 6. Jesus’ Exclusive Claim — Confirmed by the Apostles
Jesus repeatedly claimed to be the only way:

- “I am the door. If anyone enters by me, he will be saved…” — **John 10:9**
- “Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life; whoever does not obey the Son shall not see life…” — **John 3:36**

The early church understood this clearly:

> “And there is salvation in no one else, for there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved.”  
> — **Acts 4:12**

---

### 7. How We Come to the Father
Jesus is not one way among many — He is the **only** way. We come to the Father through:
- Repentance and faith in Jesus’ finished work (**Acts 20:21**)
- Receiving Him as Lord and Savior (**John 1:12**)
- Being born again by the Holy Spirit (**John 3:3-7**)

Salvation is entirely by grace through faith, not works:

> “For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast.”  
> — **Ephesians 2:8-9**

---

### Conclusion
From the moment of the Fall in **Genesis 3**, God planned to rescue humanity through His Son. The prophets foretold it. The Law proved we could not save ourselves. Only Jesus, by His sinless life and sacrificial death, could satisfy God’s justice and open the way back to the Father.

When Jesus declared, “I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me” (**John 14:6**), He was telling the truth. There is no other path.
