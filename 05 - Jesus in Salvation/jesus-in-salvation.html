A. Key Greek Terms
New Testament (Greek)
1. Born again / from above — γεννηθῇ ἄνωθεν (gennēthē anōthen)
— Born again or born from above
— John 3:3 — "Unless one is born again he cannot see the kingdom of God"
2. Born of God — γεγέννηται ἐκ τοῦ θεοῦ (gegennētai ek tou theou)
— Begotten of God
— 1 John 5:1 — "Everyone who believes… has been born of God"
3. Born again (verb) — ἀναγεννάω (anagennaō)
— To beget again, cause to be born again
— 1 Peter 1:3 — "He has caused us to be born again to a living hope"
4. Regeneration — παλιγγενεσία (palingenesia)
— Rebirth, regeneration, new genesis
— Titus 3:5 — "the washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit"
5. New creation — καινὴ κτίσις (kainē ktisis)
— New creation
— 2 Corinthians 5:17 — "If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation"
6. Made alive together — συζωοποιέω (syzōopoieō)
— Made alive together with Christ
— Ephesians 2:5 — "made us alive together with Christ"

F. Jesus a Kinsman-Redeemer
What Is a Kinsman-Redeemer?
In ancient Israel, a kinsman-redeemer was a close male relative who had the responsibility (and privilege) to step in and restore what was lost for a family member in need.
Leviticus 25:25–28 — Redeeming property sold due to poverty
Leviticus 25:47–49 — Redeeming a relative sold into slavery
Deuteronomy 25:5–10 — Marrying a childless widow to preserve the family name (levirate marriage; see also the Book of Ruth)
Jesus Meets Every Requirement
1. He became our Kinsman — by taking on human flesh, Jesus is truly one of us
John 1:14 — "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us."
Hebrews 2:14–17 — "Since the children have flesh and blood, he too shared in their humanity… He had to be made like his brothers in every way, in order that he might become a merciful and faithful high priest…"
2. He was able to pay the price — only a sinless God-man could redeem us from sin's debt
1 Peter 1:18–19 — "You were redeemed… not with perishable things such as silver or gold, but with the precious blood of Christ, a lamb without blemish or defect."
3. He was willing — Jesus laid down His life voluntarily
John 10:17–18 — "I lay down my life… No one takes it from me, but I lay it down of my own accord."
Galatians 3:13 — "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us."
Key Verses on Jesus and Redemption
Ephesians 1:7 — "In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, in accordance with the riches of God's grace."
Colossians 1:13–14 — "He has rescued us from the dominion of darkness and brought us into the kingdom of the Son he loves, in whom we have redemption, the forgiveness of sins."
Revelation 5:9 — "You are worthy… because you were slain, and with your blood you purchased for God persons from every tribe and language and people and nation."
Isaiah 53:5–6 — "He was pierced for our transgressions… the punishment that brought us peace was on him." (prophetic of the Messiah)
Galatians 4:4–5 — "God sent his Son, born of a woman… to redeem those under the law, that we might receive adoption to sonship."
Jesus doesn't just rescue us from sin's penalty — He restores our inheritance, adopts us into God's family, and gives us a new name and future. Like Boaz with Ruth, He brings outsiders (Gentiles included) into the covenant family.
