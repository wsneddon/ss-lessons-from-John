**# Christians and the Law**

### “For the law was given through Moses. Grace and truth were realized through Jesus Christ.” — **John 1:17**

# Introduction
One of the central themes of the New Testament is the transition from the Old Covenant under Moses to the New Covenant in Christ (Jeremiah 31:31-34; Hebrews 8:6-13).

Questions naturally arise:
* What was the purpose of the Law?
* Are Christians under the Law of Moses?
* What did Jesus fulfill?
* What role does the Holy Spirit now play?
* How should Christians understand commandments such as circumcision, dietary laws, and Sabbath keeping?

The New Testament answers these questions repeatedly by pointing believers to Jesus Christ, the New Covenant, and the life of the Spirit. Additional supporting passages include Romans 6:14 (“For sin will have no dominion over you, since you are not under law but under grace”) and Galatians 5:18 (“But if you are led by the Spirit, you are not under the law”).

---
# 1. The Law Came Through Moses — Grace and Truth Through Christ
## Key Text
> “For the law was given through Moses. Grace and truth were realized through Jesus Christ.”  
> — **John 1:17**

John presents a contrast between:
* the Law given through Moses
* the fullness of grace and truth brought through Christ

The Law revealed God’s holiness, human sin, and the need for atonement (Romans 7:12 — “So the law is holy, and the commandment is holy and righteous and good”). But the Law itself could not give life or righteousness.

## The Law Could Reveal Sin But Not Save
> “For through the law comes the knowledge of sin.”  
> — **Romans 3:20**

> “If a law had been given which could make alive, most certainly righteousness would have been of the law.”  
> — **Galatians 3:21**

Additional support: Romans 7:7 (“I would not have known sin except through the law”) and Galatians 3:10-11 (those relying on works of the law are under a curse, since no one is justified by the law).

---
# 2. Salvation Is by Grace Through Faith
## Children of God by New Birth
> “But to all who received him, to them he gave the right to become God’s children… who were born… of God.”  
> — **John 1:12-13**

Becoming part of God’s family comes through faith in Christ, spiritual rebirth, and the work of God — not through ethnicity, circumcision, Law-keeping, or human effort. See also John 3:3-8 (born again of the Spirit).

## Titus — Salvation by Mercy
> “He saved us—not by works of righteousness that we had done, but according to his mercy—through the washing of regeneration and renewing by the Holy Spirit.”  
> — **Titus 3:5**

## Ephesians — Salvation Is a Gift
> “For by grace you have been saved through faith… not of works.”  
> — **Ephesians 2:8-9**

Paul consistently argues that justification comes through faith in Christ rather than through observing the Law of Moses (Galatians 2:16; Romans 3:28 — “For we hold that one is justified by faith apart from works of the law”).

---
# 3. The Purpose of the Law — A Schoolmaster Leading to Christ
One of Paul’s clearest teachings appears in the **Epistle to the Galatians**.

## The Law Was Temporary and Preparatory
> “So that the law has become our tutor [schoolmaster/guardian] to bring us to Christ, that we might be justified by faith.”  
> — **Galatians 3:24**

> “But now that faith has come, we are no longer under a tutor.”  
> — **Galatians 3:25**

### Meaning of “Schoolmaster”
The Greek *paidagōgos* refers to a guardian or custodian assigned to supervise a child until maturity. Paul’s argument: The Law guarded Israel, exposed sin, pointed toward Christ, and was never the final means of righteousness. Now that Christ has come, believers are justified through faith and the role of the Law as covenant guardian has ended.

## Additional Galatians References
> “Before faith came, we were kept in custody under the law.”  
> — **Galatians 3:23**

> “Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law.”  
> — **Galatians 3:13**

> “For freedom Christ has set us free.”  
> — **Galatians 5:1**

Further support: Galatians 4:4-5 (God sent His Son to redeem those under the law) and Romans 10:4 (“For Christ is the end [goal/fulfillment] of the law for righteousness to everyone who believes”).

---
# 4. Christ Fulfilled the Law
## Jesus’ Statement
> “Don’t think that I came to destroy the law or the prophets. I didn’t come to destroy, but to fulfill.”  
> — **Matthew 5:17**

Jesus fulfilled the Law by perfectly obeying it, fulfilling prophecy, becoming the final sacrifice, and accomplishing what the Old Covenant pointed toward (Hebrews 10:1-14).

## Hebrews — The Shadow and Reality
> “For the law, having a shadow of the good things to come, not the very image of the things…”  
> — **Hebrews 10:1**

## Christ Is the End (Goal) of the Law
> “For Christ is the fulfillment/end of the law for righteousness to everyone who believes.”  
> — **Romans 10:4**

Additional: Colossians 2:16-17 and Hebrews 9:11-15 (Christ as the reality and mediator of a better covenant).

---
# 5. Released From the Law — The Dead Husband Illustration
Paul gives another important illustration in the **Epistle to the Romans**.

## The Marriage Illustration
> “A married woman is bound by law to her husband while he lives, but if the husband dies, she is discharged from the law concerning the husband.”  
> — **Romans 7:2**

Paul applies this spiritually:
> “You also were made dead to the law through the body of Christ.”  
> — **Romans 7:4**

## Main Point
Believers have died with Christ, been released from the covenantal authority of the Mosaic Law, and entered a new covenant relationship. We serve not “in oldness of the letter” but “in newness of the Spirit” (Romans 7:6).

## Additional Romans References
> “Now we have been discharged from the law.”  
> — **Romans 7:6**

> “Therefore there is now no condemnation to those who are in Christ Jesus.”  
> — **Romans 8:1**

Also: Romans 6:14 (not under law but under grace).

---
# 6. The Letter Kills — The Spirit Gives Life
## The New Covenant Ministry
> “For the letter kills, but the Spirit gives life.”  
> — **2 Corinthians 3:6**

Paul contrasts the old covenant written on stone (the ministry of death/condemnation) with the ministry of the Spirit.

## Romans 8 — Freedom in the Spirit
> “For the law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus made me free from the law of sin and of death.”  
> — **Romans 8:2**

## The Righteous Requirement Fulfilled in Us
> “That the ordinance of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit.”  
> — **Romans 8:4**

Additional: Ezekiel 36:26-27 (new heart and Spirit causing obedience) and Galatians 5:22-23 (fruit of the Spirit).

---
# 7. Circumcision and the Jerusalem Council
## Peter’s Statement
> “Why do you tempt God by putting a yoke on the neck of the disciples which neither our fathers nor we were able to bear?”  
> — **Acts 15:10**

## The Decision
Gentiles were not placed under the Mosaic Law. Salvation is through grace and faith in Christ (Acts 15:11, 19-20, 28-29).

## Galatians — Circumcision Cannot Justify
> “If you receive circumcision, Christ will profit you nothing.”  
> — **Galatians 5:2**

> “You are severed from Christ, you who would be justified by the law.”  
> — **Galatians 5:4**

Additional: Galatians 6:15 (“For neither circumcision counts for anything, nor uncircumcision, but a new creation”) and Philippians 3:3 (true circumcision is of the heart by the Spirit).

---
# 8. Food Laws and Cleanliness
## Jesus Declared Foods Clean
**Mark 7:18-19** — “Thus he declared all foods clean.”

## Peter’s Vision
**Acts 10:15** — “What God has cleansed, you must not call unclean.”

## Paul on Food
> “I know and am persuaded in the Lord Jesus that nothing is unclean of itself.”  
> — **Romans 14:14**

Additional: 1 Timothy 4:4-5 (“For everything created by God is good, and nothing is to be rejected if it is received with thanksgiving”).

---
# 9. Sabbath and Holy Days
## Early Christian Gathering
Believers gathered on the first day of the week (Acts 20:7; 1 Corinthians 16:2).

## Paul on Sabbaths
> “Let no one judge you in eating, or in drinking, or with respect to a feast day, or a new moon, or a Sabbath day.”  
> — **Colossians 2:16**

These were “a shadow of the things to come, but the body is Christ” (**Colossians 2:17**).

## Romans on Special Days
> “One person esteems one day as more sacred than another; another esteems every day alike.”  
> — **Romans 14:5**

Additional: Hebrews 4:9-10 (a Sabbath rest that remains for the people of God, fulfilled in Christ).

---
# 10. Christians Are Not Lawless
The New Testament does not teach moral chaos. Believers are transformed internally; obedience flows from faith and the Spirit.

## Love Fulfills the Law
> “For the whole law is fulfilled in one word: ‘You shall love your neighbor as yourself.’”  
> — **Galatians 5:14** (see also Romans 13:8-10)

## Walking by the Spirit
> “Walk by the Spirit, and you won’t fulfill the lust of the flesh.”  
> — **Galatians 5:16**

## The Law Written on the Heart
> “I will put my laws into their mind, and on their heart I will also write them.”  
> — **Hebrews 8:10** (quoting Jeremiah 31:33)

## The Law of Christ
> “Bear one another’s burdens, and so fulfill the law of Christ.”  
> — **Galatians 6:2**

Additional: 1 Corinthians 9:21 (“not being outside the law of God but under the law of Christ”) and James 1:25; 2:8 (the “perfect law of liberty” and royal law).

## Conclusion
The New Testament teaches that:
* The Law was holy and good (**Romans 7:12**)
* The Law revealed sin (**Romans 3:20**)
* The Law served as a tutor leading to Christ (**Galatians 3:24**)
* Christ fulfilled the Law (**Matthew 5:17**)
* Believers died to the Law through Christ (**Romans 7:4**)
* Salvation comes through grace and faith (**Ephesians 2:8-9**)
* The Spirit now gives life and transformation (**2 Corinthians 3:6**)

The Christian is not justified by the Law engraved on stone, but by union with Jesus Christ through the Holy Spirit (Hebrews 8:13 — the old covenant is obsolete). Believers are called to live holy lives empowered by the indwelling Spirit, fulfilling the righteous requirement of the law through love and faith.
