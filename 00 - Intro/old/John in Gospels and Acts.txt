Early Ministry
Call of John (by the Sea of Galilee)
Location: Sea of Galilee
References: Matthew 4:21–22; Mark 1:19–20; Luke 5:10
Details: Jesus calls John and his brother James son of Zebedee while they are fishing with their father Zebedee.
Sons of Thunder Named
Location: Galilee
Reference: Mark 3:17
Details: Jesus gives John and James the nickname Boanerges (“Sons of Thunder”).
Early Ministry Events
Jairus’ Daughter Raised
Location: Likely Capernaum
References: Mark 5:37; Luke 8:51
Details: John is part of the inner circle (Peter, James, John) allowed to witness the miracle.
Mission of the Twelve
Location: Various towns in Galilee
References: Matthew 10; Mark 6:7; Luke 9:1
Details: John is sent out with the other apostles.
Samaritan Village Rejection
Location: Samaria
Reference: Luke 9:51–56
Details: John and James ask to call down fire from heaven; Jesus rebukes them.
Mid-Ministry
The Transfiguration
Location: Traditionally Mount Tabor (or possibly Hermon)
References: Matthew 17:1–9; Mark 9:2–10; Luke 9:28–36
Details: John witnesses Jesus’ glory along with Peter and James.
“Who Is the Greatest?” / Child in Their Midst
Location: Capernaum
References: Mark 9:33–37; Luke 9:46–48
Details: John participates in discussions about greatness.
“We Forbade Him” (Unauthorized Exorcist)
Location: Likely Galilee
Reference: Luke 9:49–50
Details: John tells Jesus they tried to stop someone casting out demons in His name.
Journey to Jerusalem
Request for Seats of Honor
Location: On the road to Jerusalem
References: Matthew 20:20–28; Mark 10:35–45
Details: John (with James, via their mother in Matthew) asks for positions of honor in Christ’s kingdom.
Passion Week
Preparation for the Last Supper
Location: Jerusalem
Reference: Luke 22:8
Details: Jesus sends Peter and John to prepare the Passover meal.
The Last Supper (Leaning on Jesus’ Breast)
Location: Upper Room, Jerusalem
References: John 13:23–25
Details: John is “the disciple whom Jesus loved,” reclining next to Jesus.
In the Garden of Gethsemane
Location: Garden of Gethsemane
References: Matthew 26:36–37; Mark 14:32–33
Details: John is among the three closest disciples during Jesus’ agony.
At the High Priest’s Courtyard
Location: Jerusalem
Reference: John 18:15–16
Details: John (likely the “other disciple”) gains access to the courtyard during Jesus’ trial.
At the Cross
Location: Golgotha
Reference: John 19:25–27
Details: Jesus entrusts His mother Mary to John.
Resurrection Events
The Empty Tomb
Location: Tomb near Jerusalem
Reference: John 20:2–8
Details: John runs with Peter to the tomb and believes upon seeing it empty.
Appearance by the Sea (Post-Resurrection)
Location: Sea of Galilee
Reference: John 21:7
Details: John recognizes the risen Jesus first.
In the Book of Acts
Waiting in the Upper Room
Location: Jerusalem
Reference: Acts 1:13
Details: John is listed among the apostles after the ascension.
Healing of the Lame Man
Location: Temple in Jerusalem
Reference: Acts 3:1–11
Details: John accompanies Peter when the man is healed.
Before the Sanhedrin
Location: Jerusalem
References: Acts 4:1–22
Details: John stands boldly with Peter before Jewish leaders.
Ministry in Samaria
Location: Samaria
Reference: Acts 8:14–17
Details: John and Peter lay hands on new believers to receive the Holy Spirit.
Summary Insight

John consistently appears:

As part of the inner circle (Peter, James, John)
In key revelation moments (Transfiguration, Resurrection)
As a witness to the Passion
As an early leader in the Jerusalem church in Acts
