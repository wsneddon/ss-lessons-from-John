### 1. Rapid Geological Formations
- **Polystrate fossils**: Trees and animal remains found vertically spanning multiple sedimentary layers supposedly deposited over millions of years.
- **Fossil graveyards**: Massive concentrations of rapidly buried creatures (e.g., dinosaur bone beds, fish shoals).
- **Soft tissue in dinosaur fossils**: Original proteins, blood vessels, and cells in bones dated to 65+ million years old (e.g., Mary Schweitzer’s *T. rex* finds).
- **Rapid canyon formation**: Observations of canyons (e.g., Mount St. Helens 1980 eruption) forming in days or weeks, not millions of years.
- **Flat gaps / paraconformities**: Missing supposed millions of years with little to no erosion between sedimentary layers.

### 2. Radiometric Dating Issues
- **Inconsistent dates**: Different radiometric methods applied to the same rock often yield wildly different ages.
- **Excess helium in zircons**: Zircon crystals contain high levels of helium from uranium decay. Helium diffuses out quickly, suggesting only thousands of years of decay rather than billions (RATE project findings).
- **Carbon-14 in “ancient” materials**: Detectable C-14 in diamonds, coal, and fossils claimed to be millions of years old (C-14 half-life of ~5,730 years limits detectable levels to roughly <50,000–100,000 years maximum).
- **Accelerated decay evidence**: Proposals of past periods of rapid nuclear decay during the creation week or the Flood.

### 3. Biological and Genetic Evidence
- **Human population growth**: Current population growth rates, when extrapolated backward, fit a starting population of roughly 8 people (Noah’s family) about 4,500 years ago better than millions of years of human history.
- **Genetic entropy / mutation load**: Accumulation of harmful mutations suggests genomes are degrading rapidly, which is inconsistent with deep time (arguments advanced by John Sanford).
- **Living fossils**: Many species (e.g., coelacanth, horseshoe crab) appear essentially unchanged from fossils claimed to be hundreds of millions of years old.
- **Lack of transitional forms**: Perceived scarcity or ongoing controversy over clear evolutionary intermediates in the fossil record.

### 4. Astronomical and Cosmological Arguments
- **Comet lifetimes**: Short-period comets lose material quickly and should not exist after millions of years without a constant source (Oort cloud hypothesized but unobserved).
- **Magnetic field decay**: Earth’s magnetic field is decaying at a measurable rate; extrapolating backward, it would have been impossibly strong only tens of thousands of years ago.
- **Spiral galaxy arms**: Galaxies should wind up tightly after a few hundred million years, yet many retain clear spiral structure.
- **Moon recession**: The Moon is moving away from Earth; backward extrapolation suggests it would have been catastrophically close to Earth within roughly 1–2 billion years (not 4.5 billion).

### 5. Salt in the Ocean
- Sodium input into the oceans from rivers exceeds removal rates. At current rates, the oceans should be much saltier if Earth were billions of years old.

### 6. Sediment and Erosion Rates
- Continents would erode down to sea level in roughly 10–20 million years at current erosion rates.
- Ocean sediment thickness is far less than expected for hundreds of millions of years of accumulation.

### 7. Evidence for Noah's Flood
- Many ancient cultures have flood legends remarkably similar to the account of Noah’s Flood.
- Early historical records and civilizations appear suddenly around 3000–4000 BC with advanced knowledge, with no clear evidence of millions of years of gradual primitive development.
