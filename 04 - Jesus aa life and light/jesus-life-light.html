Opening Passage — John 1:1–5
John 1:1–5 — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was in the beginning with God. All things were made through him, and without him was not any thing made that was made. In him was life, and the life was the light of men. The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it."
A. Key Greek and Hebrew Words
Old Testament (Hebrew)
1. Light — אוֹר (ʾôr)
— Illumination, guidance, joy, salvation, and God's presence
— Genesis 1:3 — "Let there be light"; Psalm 27:1 — "The LORD is my light"
2. Life — חַיִּים (ḥayyîm)
— Vitality, full and abundant life; often covenantal blessing (from root ḥayah — "to live")
— Deuteronomy 30:20 — "For he is your life"
3. Breath of Life — נְשָׁמָה (neshamah)
— Life-giving breath from God
— Genesis 2:7 — God breathes the breath of life into Adam
New Testament (Greek)
1. Light — φῶς (phōs)
— Spiritual truth, revelation, purity, divine presence
— Contrasts with σκότος (skotos) = darkness (ignorance, sin, evil)
— John 1:4–5 — "the life was the light of men. The light shines in the darkness"
— John 8:12 — "I am the light of the world"
2. Life — ζωή (zōē)
— Divine, eternal, abundant life — the very life of God
— Distinct from βίος (bios) = biological life
— John 1:4 — "In him was life"
— John 10:10 — "I came that they may have life and have it abundantly"
3. Word — λόγος (logos)
— Word, reason, divine expression — key in John 1
— John 1:1 — "In the beginning was the Word"
4. Spirit — πνεῦμα (pneuma)
— Spirit, breath, wind — associated with giving life
— John 3:5–6 — "That which is born of the Spirit is spirit"

D. The Relationship Between Light and Life
1. Foundational linking passage
a. Psalm 36:9 — "With you is the fountain of life; in your light do we see light."
2. John's theology (John 1:4)
a. Life (zōē) = Divine essence — the source
b. Light (phōs) = Divine revelation — the expression
c. Life produces light; light reveals life
3. A repeating biblical pattern
a. God gives life → life produces light → light defeats darkness → darkness represents death
b. Seen in: Genesis 1 → Psalm 36 → John 1 → Revelation 21–22
4. Creation parallel
a. Genesis 1: Light created first; life follows
b. John 1: Word → Life → Light — strong theological parallel
5. Eternal fulfillment
a. Revelation 21:23 — "The glory of God gives it light, and its lamp is the Lamb."
b. Revelation 22:1–2 — River of life, tree of life, eternal light — life and light completely united.
Further Study: Christian Thought on Light and Life
1. Athanasius (On the Incarnation, 4th c.)
— Christ restores life by bringing light; darkness represents corruption and death; light restores creation
2. Augustine (4th–5th c.)
— Light = truth and knowledge of God; ignorance = darkness; union with God = life
3. John Calvin
— Light → revelation; Life → regeneration; the Spirit provides both illumination and new life
