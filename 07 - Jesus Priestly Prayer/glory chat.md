# Glory in Scripture

## A Lesson Beginning with John 17

John 17 is one of the most profound chapters in Scripture for understanding glory. In Jesus' prayer, we see:

1. The eternal glory of the Father.
2. The glory shared between the Father and the Son before creation.
3. The glory given to believers.
4. The ultimate destiny of God's people—to behold and share in God's glory.

> "Father, glorify Me in Your own presence with the glory that I had with You before the world existed." (John 17:5)

> "The glory that You have given Me I have given to them." (John 17:22)

This chapter serves as a gateway into the Bible's entire theology of glory.

---

# Word Study

## Hebrew (Old Testament)

### כָּבוֹד (kabod)

**Literal meaning:** weight, heaviness, substance, importance

**English concepts:**

* Honor
* Splendor
* Majesty
* Reputation
* Worth

In ordinary Hebrew usage, something "weighty" was important and significant. A person with "kabod" was someone of substance and honor.

### Scriptural Meaning

When applied to God, *kabod* refers to the visible and invisible manifestation of His greatness, holiness, power, and presence.

Examples:

* Exodus 24:16-17
* Exodus 33:18-23
* Isaiah 6:1-3
* Ezekiel 1

God's glory is not merely His reputation; it is the manifestation of who He truly is.

---

## Greek (New Testament)

### δόξα (doxa)

Originally in classical Greek:

* Opinion
* Reputation
* Esteem

In the Septuagint (Greek Old Testament), *doxa* became the standard translation for *kabod*.

Thus in the New Testament it came to mean:

* Divine splendor
* Honor
* Majesty
* Radiance
* The revealed excellence of God

Examples:

* John 1:14
* John 17
* Romans 8:18
* 2 Corinthians 4:17
* Revelation 21:23

# Common Modern Usage vs Biblical Usage

## Modern

Today "glory" often means:

* Fame
* Praise
* Recognition
* Personal achievement

Examples:

* "Glory days"
* "Winning glory"

The emphasis is usually on human accomplishment.

---

## Biblical

In Scripture, glory means:

### God's intrinsic greatness

God possesses glory whether anyone recognizes it or not.

> "The heavens declare the glory of God."
> (Psalm 19:1)

### God's manifested presence

Glory often appears visibly:

* Cloud
* Fire
* Light
* Radiance

### God's worth and honor

The proper response to glory is worship.

### The destiny of redeemed humanity

Believers are called to share in God's glory.

# The Glory of the Father

## Old Testament

### Creation Reveals His Glory

> Psalm 19:1

Creation is a display of God's greatness.

---

### The Glory Cloud

Examples:

* Exodus 16:10
* Exodus 24:16-17
* Exodus 40:34-35
* 1 Kings 8:10-11

God's glory fills the Tabernacle and Temple.

---

### Moses and God's Glory

Exodus 33:18

> "Show me Your glory."

God responds by revealing His character:

> "The LORD, the LORD, compassionate and gracious..."
> (Exodus 34:6)

An important biblical principle emerges:

**God's glory is not merely brightness—it is the revelation of His character.**

---

### Isaiah's Vision

Isaiah 6:3

> "The whole earth is full of His glory."

God's glory fills creation because His reign extends everywhere.

# The Glory of Jesus Christ

## Before Creation

John 17:5

Jesus possessed glory with the Father before the world existed.

This is one of the strongest declarations of Christ's preexistence.

---

## Glory in the Incarnation

John 1:14

> "We have seen His glory."

Jesus reveals God's glory in human flesh.

Unlike Sinai's fire and cloud, God's glory now appears in a person.

---

## Glory in His Works

John repeatedly calls miracles "signs."

Examples:

* Water into wine (John 2:11)
* Raising Lazarus (John 11:4)

These reveal Christ's glory.

---

## Glory Through the Cross

A surprising New Testament theme:

The cross is glory.

John 12:23

> "The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified."

The world sees humiliation.

God sees supreme revelation of:

* Love
* Justice
* Mercy
* Obedience

---

## Glory Through Resurrection

Romans 6:4

Christ is raised through the Father's glory.

---

## Glory Through Exaltation

Philippians 2:9-11

After His obedience unto death, Christ is exalted above every name.

---

# The Holy Spirit and Glory

The Spirit is not often called "glory" directly, but He is intimately connected with glory.

## Spirit Reveals Glory

2 Corinthians 3:18

> "We all...beholding the glory of the Lord, are being transformed..."

The transformation occurs through the Spirit.

---

## Spirit as the Presence of God

In the Old Testament:

* Glory filled the Tabernacle.
* Glory filled the Temple.

In the New Testament:

The Spirit fills believers.

This suggests the Spirit mediates God's glorious presence among His people.

---

## Spirit of Glory

1 Peter 4:14

> "The Spirit of glory and of God rests upon you."

One of the clearest links between the Holy Spirit and glory.

# The Glory Given to God's People

This is one of the most astonishing biblical themes.

## Shared Glory

John 17:22

> "The glory that You have given Me I have given to them."

Not equality with God.

Rather:

* Union with Christ
* Participation in God's life
* Reflection of God's character

---

## From Glory to Glory

2 Corinthians 3:18

Believers are progressively transformed into Christ's image.

---

## Future Glory

Romans 8:18

> "The sufferings of this present time are not worth comparing with the glory that is to be revealed to us."

---

## Glorified Bodies

Philippians 3:21

Believers receive bodies like Christ's glorious resurrection body.

---

## Seeing His Glory

John 17:24

Jesus' ultimate request:

> "That they may be with Me where I am, to see My glory."

The final destiny of believers is not merely heaven.

It is beholding Christ.

# Glory and Oneness in John 17

A theme especially connected to your previous study of **ἕν (hen, "one")**.

Notice the sequence:

1. The Father glorifies the Son.
2. The Son glorifies the Father.
3. The Son gives glory to believers.
4. That glory produces unity.

John 17:22-23

> "The glory that You have given Me I have given to them, that they may be one."

Glory is therefore not merely radiance.

It is participation in the divine life and love that eternally exists between Father and Son.

The unity of the Church flows from sharing in that glory.

# C.S. Lewis and *The Weight of Glory*

Few modern writers captured the biblical vision of glory better than C. S. Lewis.

You can find information about his famous sermon and essay collection *The Weight of Glory* here:

* [The Weight of Glory (Open Library)](https://openlibrary.org/works/OL71133W/The_Weight_of_Glory?edition=weightofgloryoth00lewi&utm_source=chatgpt.com)
* [Bearing the Weight of Glory (C.S. Lewis Institute)](https://www.cslewisinstitute.org/bearing-the-weight-of-glory/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Lewis argued that the deepest meaning of glory is not primarily splendor, but God's approval and delight in His redeemed children. He connected this to passages such as Romans 8 and John 17, where believers are invited into the very life and joy of God. ([C.S. Lewis Institute][1])

A fitting summary from John 17 would be:

> The Father's glory is revealed in the Son.
>
> The Son glorifies the Father through obedience, death, and resurrection.
>
> The Spirit reveals and imparts that glory.
>
> The chosen are transformed by that glory, united through it, and will one day behold it fully.
