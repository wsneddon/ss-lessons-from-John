# Glory in Scripture

## A Lesson Beginning with John 17

John 17 is one of the most profound chapters in Scripture for understanding glory. In Jesus' prayer, we see:

1. The eternal glory of the Father.
2. The glory shared between the Father and the Son before creation.
3. The glory given to believers.
4. The ultimate destiny of God's people — to behold and share in God's glory.

> "Father, the hour has come; glorify your Son that the Son may glorify you." (John 17:1)

> "Father, glorify Me in Your own presence with the glory that I had with You before the world existed." (John 17:5)

> "The glory that You have given Me I have given to them, that they may be one even as we are one." (John 17:22)

Glory here is not abstract but relational, missional, and tied to the cross and resurrection — the "hour" of Jesus' glorification through suffering and victory. This chapter serves as a gateway into the Bible's entire theology of glory.

---

# Word Study

## Hebrew (Old Testament)

### כָּבוֹד (kabod / kavod)

**Root:** *kābed* — "to be heavy" or "weighty"

**Literal meaning:** Weight, heaviness, substance, importance

**English concepts:**
- Honor, splendor, majesty, reputation, worth, wealth, magnificence

In ordinary Hebrew usage, something "weighty" was important and significant. A person with *kabod* was someone of substance and honor. It conveys something of great value that demands respect — often terrifying in its intensity. The opposite is *qālôn* (lightness, shame, dishonor).

**When applied to God,** *kabod* refers to the visible and invisible manifestation of His greatness, holiness, power, and presence — not merely His reputation but the manifestation of who He truly is.

**Key references:**
- Exodus 16:10; 24:16–17 — glory appearing as cloud and fire
- Exodus 40:34–35 — glory fills the Tabernacle, preventing entry
- Isaiah 6:1–3 — seraphim cry "Holy, holy, holy; the whole earth is full of His glory"
- Ezekiel 1; 10; 43 — Ezekiel's visions of the glory departing and returning
- Psalm 19:1 — "The heavens declare the glory of God"
- Psalm 29:1–2 — calls to give God glory

---

## Greek (New Testament)

### δόξα (doxa)

**Root:** *dokeō* — "to think, seem, or opine"

**Classical Greek meaning:** Opinion, reputation, esteem

In the Septuagint (Greek Old Testament), *doxa* became the standard translation for *kabod*, absorbing its full range of meaning. Thus in the New Testament it came to mean:
- Divine splendor, honor, majesty, radiance
- The revealed excellence and manifested character of God

*Doxa* ties to *doxazō* — "to glorify, to honor."

**Key references:**
- Luke 2:9 — the glory of the Lord shining around the shepherds at Jesus' birth
- John 1:14 — "We have seen His glory"
- John 17 — the glory prayer
- Hebrews 1:3 — Jesus as "the radiance of the glory of God"
- 2 Corinthians 3:18 — believers transformed from glory to glory
- Revelation 21:23 — God's glory illuminates the New Jerusalem

---

# Common Uses vs. Biblical Understanding

| | Modern / Classical | Biblical |
|---|---|---|
| Meaning | Fame, praise, recognition, personal achievement | The weight and worth of God's perfect character put on display |
| Focus | Human accomplishment and reputation | God-centered — His intrinsic excellence, worthy of worship |
| Examples | "Glory days," winning glory, vainglory | Tabernacle glory, Christ's resurrection, shared glory in John 17 |

**Key distinction:** Biblical glory is not self-promotion. It is the public display of divine perfection — often linked to light, presence, and redemptive acts. Humanity falls short of it (Romans 3:23) but is invited to participate through Christ. True glory is found in giving it to God, not seeking it for ourselves (Philippians 2:3).

# The Glory of the Father

## Creation Reveals His Glory

> "The heavens declare the glory of God." (Psalm 19:1)

Creation is not merely beautiful — it is a constant display of God's greatness. His glory fills the entire earth (Isaiah 6:3; Numbers 14:21; Habakkuk 2:14).

---

## The Glory Cloud

God's glory filled the Tabernacle and Temple with visible, physical weight:

- Exodus 16:10 — glory appears in the cloud
- Exodus 24:16–17 — glory settles on Sinai like devouring fire
- Exodus 40:34–35 — glory fills the Tabernacle; Moses cannot enter
- 1 Kings 8:10–11 — glory fills Solomon's Temple

---

## Moses and God's Glory

Exodus 33:18 — Moses asks: "Show me Your glory."

God responds not with blinding light alone, but with the revelation of His character:

> "The LORD, the LORD, compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, and abounding in lovingkindness and truth." (Exodus 34:6)

**An important biblical principle:** God's glory is not merely brightness — it is the revelation of His character. Holiness, love, justice, and power are all part of it.

---

## Isaiah's Vision

Isaiah 6:1–3 — The seraphim cry:

> "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD of hosts; the whole earth is full of His glory."

God's glory fills creation because His reign extends everywhere. The Father's glory is jealous for His own name (Isaiah 42:8; 48:11) and will ultimately be known throughout the earth.

---

## The Father's Glory in John 17

Jesus seeks to glorify the Father through His obedience and finished work (vv. 1, 4). The Father's glory is both the source and the goal. Eternal life itself is defined as knowing the Father and the Son (v. 3).

> "I glorified You on the earth, having accomplished the work which You have given Me to do." (John 17:4)

---

# The Glory of Jesus Christ

## Before Creation

John 17:5 — Jesus prays:

> "Father, glorify Me in Your own presence with the glory that I had with You before the world existed."

This is one of the strongest declarations of Christ's preexistence in all of Scripture. The glory is not new — it is the restoration of what was veiled in the Incarnation.

---

## Glory in the Incarnation

John 1:14

> "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we have seen His glory, glory as of the only Son from the Father, full of grace and truth."

Unlike Sinai's cloud and fire, God's glory now appears in a person. Jesus is "the radiance of the glory of God and the exact imprint of His nature" (Hebrews 1:3) — not merely reflecting glory but embodying it.

---

## Glory in His Works

John repeatedly calls miracles "signs" — they reveal who Jesus is:

- Water into wine (John 2:11) — "manifested His glory, and His disciples believed in Him"
- Raising Lazarus (John 11:4) — "This sickness is not to end in death, but for the glory of God, so that the Son of God may be glorified by it"

---

## Glory Through the Cross

A surprising New Testament theme: the cross is glory.

John 12:23 — as the hour of death approaches:

> "The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified."

John 13:31–32 — after Judas departs:

> "Now is the Son of Man glorified, and God is glorified in Him. If God is glorified in Him, God will also glorify Him in Himself, and will glorify Him immediately."

The world sees humiliation. God sees the supreme revelation of:
- Love
- Justice
- Mercy
- Obedience

The cross redefines glory as sacrificial love rather than worldly power.

---

## Glory Through Resurrection

Romans 6:4 — Christ is raised through the Father's glory. The resurrection is the Father's vindication and glorification of the Son.

---

## Glory Through Exaltation

Philippians 2:9–11 — after His obedience unto death:

> "God highly exalted Him, and bestowed on Him the name which is above every name, so that at the name of Jesus every knee will bow... and every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father."

---

# The Holy Spirit and Glory

The Spirit is not named in John 17, but the prayer is deeply Trinitarian, and Scripture connects the Spirit intimately to glory.

## The Spirit Glorifies Jesus

John 16:14 — Jesus teaches:

> "He [the Spirit] will glorify Me, for He will take of Mine and will disclose it to you."

The Spirit's ministry is not self-promotion but the magnification of Christ.

---

## The Spirit Transforms Believers into Glory

2 Corinthians 3:18:

> "We all, with unveiled face, beholding the glory of the Lord, are being transformed into the same image from one degree of glory to another. For this comes from the Lord who is the Spirit."

Beholding leads to becoming. The Spirit mediates this transformation.

---

## The Spirit as God's Indwelling Presence

In the Old Testament, God's glory filled the Tabernacle and Temple. In the New Testament, the Spirit fills believers — making their bodies the new temple of God's glorious presence (1 Corinthians 6:19; Ephesians 2:22).

---

## The Spirit of Glory

1 Peter 4:14 — one of the clearest direct links:

> "The Spirit of glory and of God rests upon you."

The Spirit is both a present foretaste and the guarantee of future glory (Ephesians 1:13–14; Romans 8:11).

---

# The Glory Given to God's People

This is one of the most astonishing biblical themes.

## Shared Glory — John 17:22

> "The glory that You have given Me I have given to them, that they may be one even as we are one."

Not equality with God. Rather:
- Union with Christ
- Participation in God's life
- Reflection of God's character

Notice the sequence:

1. The Father glorifies the Son.
2. The Son glorifies the Father.
3. The Son gives glory to believers.
4. That shared glory produces unity.

Glory is therefore not merely radiance. It is participation in the divine life and love that eternally exists between Father and Son. **The unity of the Church flows from sharing in that glory.**

---

## From Glory to Glory

2 Corinthians 3:18 — transformation is not instantaneous but progressive. Believers are being changed into Christ's image — from one degree of glory to another — by the Spirit.

---

## Future Glory

Romans 8:17–18:

> "If children, heirs also, heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ, if indeed we suffer with Him so that we may also be glorified with Him. For I consider that the sufferings of this present time are not worth comparing with the glory that is to be revealed to us."

Romans 8:29–30 — the golden chain: foreknown, predestined, called, justified, **glorified**. Glorification is as certain as foreknowledge.

---

## Glorified Bodies

Philippians 3:21 — believers will receive bodies transformed to be like Christ's glorious resurrection body. Colossians 3:4 — "When Christ, who is our life, is revealed, then you also will be revealed with Him in glory."

---

## Seeing His Glory — The Final Goal

John 17:24 — Jesus' ultimate request:

> "Father, I desire that they also, whom You have given Me, be with Me where I am, so that they may see My glory which You have given Me."

The final destiny of believers is not merely heaven as a place. It is beholding Christ — face to face, in full glory, forever.

1 John 3:2 — "We will see Him just as He is." Revelation 21:23 — the New Jerusalem needs no sun or moon because the glory of God illuminates it, and its lamp is the Lamb.

---

# The Story of Glory Through the Bible

| Stage | What Happens | Key Passage |
|---|---|---|
| **Creation** | Humanity made in God's image; crowned with glory and honor | Psalm 8; Genesis 1:26–27 |
| **Fall** | Humanity falls short of God's glory | Romans 3:23 |
| **Redemption** | Jesus reveals God's glory and restores humanity | John 1:14 |
| **Sanctification** | The Spirit transforms believers from glory to glory | 2 Corinthians 3:18 |
| **Consummation** | Believers share in Christ's glory forever | Romans 8:30; Revelation 21:23 |

# C.S. Lewis and *The Weight of Glory*

Few modern writers captured the biblical vision of glory better than C. S. Lewis in his 1941 sermon "The Weight of Glory."

Lewis argued that the deepest meaning of glory is not primarily splendor, but **God's approval and delight in His redeemed children**. He connected this to passages such as Romans 8 and John 17, where believers are invited into the very life and joy of God.

A fitting summary from the Bible's theology of glory:

> The Father's glory is revealed in the Son.
>
> The Son glorifies the Father through obedience, death, and resurrection.
>
> The Spirit reveals and imparts that glory.
>
> The chosen are transformed by that glory, united through it, and will one day behold it fully.

# Discussion Questions

1. Moses asked to see God's glory, and God revealed His character (Exodus 33–34). What does that teach us about what glory really is?
2. John 17:22 says Jesus gave His glory to believers. What does it look like practically to "have" Christ's glory?
3. If the Spirit is transforming us "from glory to glory" (2 Corinthians 3:18), what does that process look like in everyday life?
4. The cross is described as Jesus' hour of glory. How does that redefine how we think about suffering and glory in our own lives?
5. Jesus' final request was that believers *behold* His glory (John 17:24). What does that hope mean to you personally?

---

*Meditate on John 17 alongside Exodus 33–34, Isaiah 6, 2 Corinthians 3–4, and Revelation 21–22 for a fuller picture. Glory is the thread of God's story — from creation to new creation.*
